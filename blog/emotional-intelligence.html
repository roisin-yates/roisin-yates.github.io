Emotional Intelligence

Mon 23 Jan 2021

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (or Emotional Quotient/EQ) is the ability to recognise, manage and understand emotions within yourself and other people. Motivation may also be included: having clear goals and working towards them indicates an understanding of yourself and a willingness to better yourself.

There are different levels, or stages, of emotional intelligence:

  1. Identification: Identifying and understanding emotional signals, including verbal (phrasing, tone, volume) and non-verbal (body language, facial expressions) in yourself and in others.
  2. Feeling: Allowing yourself and others to feel and experience emotions rather than suppressing them
  3. Understanding: Understanding how emotions can affect the environment around you, both negatively and positively
  4. Managing: Responding and regulating the emotions. Knowing your cues and reactions and managing them in the way you know works for you. Helping others to manage their emotions - if someone wants to be comforted, comfort them so they can work through their emotions. If someone wants to be alone, give them space etc.

How is Emotional Intelligence different from IQ

Intelligence Quotient(IQ):

Reflects your logical and analytical abilities, including:

  • Problem solving
  • Strategising
  • Use of language and numbers
  • Spacial awareness
  • Memory

Emotional Intelligence/EQ:

Reflects your understanding and management of emotions, including:

  • Awareness of others around you
  • Empathy
  • Communication and Co-operation
  • Management of stress
  • Mindfulness

Measured by:

IQ can be measured through multiple tests that incorporate puzzles, mathematic equations and patterns. Popular tests include: the Stanford-Binet Intelligence scale and the Wechsler Adult intelligence scale.

EQ can be measured through different personality tests, either filled out by yourself to evaluate your own abilities, or filled out by people close to you who can interpret your behaviour.


Bias:

Both IQ and EQ are impacted by environmental conditions around you. IQ can be impacted by socio-ecnomic status, education and health. EQ can be impacted by psychological health, trauma, culture and upbringing


Development

IQ has traditionally been seen as static and unchanging, but it is now theorised that IQ can be improved by working on puzzles, learning a new skill (e.g musical instrument) and learning a new language.

EQ can be developed through self-reflection, such as meditation, journalling and communication.

Why is EQ important

Whilst IQ was traditionally seen as more important for success, it is now shown that EQ plays a more important role. A Forbes study showed that 90% of the top performers had a high EQ.

Emotional intelligence is very important for good communication and relationship building skills. It is a crucial skill for leadership in any career. It can help with stress management, teamwork and conflict management.
